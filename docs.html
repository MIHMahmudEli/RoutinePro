📋 Queue

Managing the Course Queue

Every course you add appears as a card in the left sidebar. Here's everything you can do with them.

Adding a Course

Click any result in the search dropdown. The course instantly appears at the top of your sidebar queue. You can add as many courses as you need.

Removing a Course

Hover over a course card and click the ✕ icon that appears in the top-right corner. This removes it from your queue and regeneration will no longer include it.

Switching Sections

Each card has a section dropdown at the bottom. Use it to preview or select a specific section before generating. The grid updates live.

Pinning a Section

Click the 🔒 lock icon on a card to "pin" that specific section. The generator will always use this exact section — it won't test alternatives for this course.

🔀 Sections

Switching Sections

Each course can have multiple sections with different teachers, rooms, and schedules. You're in full control.

How Sections Work

A course like "Data Structures" might have 8 sections (Sec 1 through 8), each with a different day/time. When you generate, the engine tries all sections to find conflict-free combos — unless you've pinned a specific one.

✏️ Manual Entry

Manual Entry Mode

Have a class that isn't in the database? Add it yourself — thesis defence, off-campus lab, guest lectures, anything.

How to use Manual Entry

Click "Manual Mode" button in the left panel

1

Click "+ Manual Mode" button to switch from Search mode to Manual Entry form.

2

Fill in the Subject Name, choose days with checkboxes, set a start & end time, and optionally a room number.

3

Click "Add to Routine". The custom block is added to your queue and protected from conflicts just like any other course.

4

You can edit a manual course later by clicking the ✏️ pencil icon on its card, or remove it with ✕.

Press Enter in any text field to submit quickly. The app will auto-suggest an end time (+90 minutes) when you set a start time.

⚙️ Parameters

Filters & Parameters

These filters control which sections the generator is even allowed to include. Set them before generating.

All Filter Options

FilterWhat it doesExample
🕐 Start Time No class can start before this time 08:00 AM — excludes any section before 8am
🕔 End Time No class can end after this time 05:00 PM — won't show classes after 5pm
👥 Min Seats Minimum available seats required 5 seats — hides nearly-full sections
📅 Day Filter Only use sections on checked days Uncheck Saturday to avoid weekend classes
🏷️ Status Filter Which section statuses are acceptable Check only "Open" to exclude Reserved/Blocked/Freshman
📊 Sort By How results are ordered "Min Gap" puts tightest schedules first

Too strict = no results. If you set very tight filters (e.g., 9AM–12PM only, Open sections only), the engine may find 0 valid scenarios. Ease up on one filter at a time until results appear.

☁️ Portal Sync

Keeping Data Current

RoutinePro has two data sources: a global cloud database and your own personal upload. Here's how each works.

Global Database

Live

Maintained by admins. Automatically loaded for every new visitor. Always contains the latest semester data. Updated via the Admin Portal.

Your Local Upload

Personal

Upload your own file from the university portal. Supports both .xlsx and .json. Your local upload overrides the global data and persists in your browser until you clear it.

How to Upload Your Own Data

1

Click the ☁️ refresh/cloud icon in the top header to open the Portal Sync panel.

2

Download your Offered Course Report from your university portal as an .xlsx file.

3

Click "Upload File" and select that file. The app parses it, auto-detects the semester name, and reloads.

4

The Last Sync timestamp updates and you'll see your course count in the sync panel header.

Want to revert to the global database? Click "Sync from Cloud" in the sync panel. This clears your local upload and fetches the latest global data.

❓ FAQ

Frequently Asked
Questions

Why does the generator show 0 results?

Your filters are too strict. Try widening the time range, reducing the minimum seat count, or enabling more day checkboxes. If you still get 0 results, the combination of pinned sections physically cannot co-exist without conflicts.

My course isn't showing up in search — what do I do?

The global database might not have the latest semester data yet. Upload your own .xlsx file via Portal Sync to get the most current course list from your university portal directly.

Will my courses be saved if I close the browser?

Course data and your local sync file are persisted in localStorage, so they survive browser restarts on the same device. However, your selected courses queue is session-based and will reset on page reload by design.

The PNG export looks different from what I see on screen

The export engine adds metadata overlays (semester, gap, branding) that aren't visible in the live view. This is by design. If you use Focus Mode, the export also applies the cropped layout. The colors are theme-faithful but may slightly vary due to canvas rendering.

What does the 🌙 Ramadan button not appearing mean?

Ramadan Mode is globally controlled by the administrator. The button is hidden outside of the Ramadan period. When enabled by the admin, it will appear automatically in the header for all users.
